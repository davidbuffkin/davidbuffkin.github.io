    • Greenhouse Simulator

    What you're seeing is a realistic, fully-interactive simulation of a plant terrarium.

    Features

    The simulation program is designed to be integrated into a ROS network where an autonomous agent can control various aspects of the simulation (the actuators are a water pump, fans, and light) based on information it recieves (the sensors are temperature, humidity, soil moisture, etc.). In this way, it is meant to be used to test the autonomous growth of plants. The simulation of the environment is robust and responsive, taking into account the time of day for sunlight, modelling real-world hydrologic interactions, growing the plants realistically based on the simulated environment, and much more.

    My Work

    Everything you see I wrote or created. I created the 3D models for the terrarium its parts in Blender, I wrote the python necessary to render them, I linked the interaction of the program to ROS and other agents, and I modelled the mathematics necessary to make the plants grow realistically - both the geometry involved in shaping the plants, and the interaction with environmental factors to adjust plant growth.

    Spotiface

    Get a music playlist based on how you feel.

    Features

    Don't know what you want to listen to? Spotiface does. Just take a picture of your face and Spotiface will generate you a Spotify playlist based on your current mood and your preexisting music taste. It's quick and easy-just snap a picture and the face analysis will automatically process-and your unique playlist will show up right in your Spotify account ready for you to enjoy. And, if you want music you haven't heard before, just close your eyes and we will pick some unseen music for you!

    Music is best enjoyed when it fits your mood. Now, you can enjoy music anytime, because it always fits your mood.

    My Work

    Spotiface was the product of HackMIT 2020 by me and three friends from high school. Generally, we all worked at least a bit on each part, but I specialized in getting the face detection setup and integrated, linking with the Spotify web API, the playlist generation algorithm, and a bit of design work. The app is written in React Native, using the Expo client. Check it out on GitHub.

    While at first the project was a bit of a gimmick, I have actually found it quite useful, and I do plan to continue working on it (or a new version) for production.
